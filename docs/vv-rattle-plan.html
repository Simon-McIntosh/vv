VV Rattle MC Analysis

§1 — Project Summary

The ITER Vacuum Vessel (VV) rests on 9 gravity supports equally spaced on a circle of radius R = 8 m. Each support constrains the VV in the toroidal direction only (radial is free for thermal expansion), with a total gap of 3 mm (±1.5 mm per support, realised by a pin/hinge perpendicular to the radial direction).

During assembly, each support lands at an independent random position within its gap. The assembly team needs to know: for a given assembly state, how much additional rigid-body motion ("rattle") is possible before the VV is blocked by the hinges?

The deliverable is bounding rattle statistics (P95, P99, worst case) in mm, with clear physical understanding and verification against degenerate cases.

§3 — Physics Model

Geometry and kinematics

The VV is a rigid body with three in-plane DOFs: (dx, dy, dθ). Support i is located at angle φᵢ = π/2 − 2πi/9 on a circle of radius R = 8 m. The toroidal unit vector is t̂ᵢ = (−sin φᵢ, cos φᵢ). The toroidal displacement of support i due to VV motion δq is: 

  δτᵢ = t̂ᵢ · (δdx, δdy) + R · δdθ  =  A[i,:] · δq

The 9×3 kinematic matrix A has rank 3 (the system is over-determined but all three rigid-body DOFs are constrained). The third column of A equals R = 8 for all supports (verified analytically).

Assembly state and gap distribution

During assembly, support i lands at toroidal position uᵢ sampled independently and uniformly from [−1.5, +1.5] mm. The key physical insight is that these 9 values are not constrained to the 3-dimensional column space of A — they are independent gap positions from manufacturing and placement tolerances.

After assembly, the remaining gap at support i is: 

  g⁺ᵢ = +1.5mm − uᵢ   (room to move in + toroidal direction)
  g⁻ᵢ = +1.5mm + uᵢ   (room to move in − toroidal direction)

These gaps are measured from the original assembly positions uᵢ, not from any projected rigid-body fit. This is critical — using the lstsq projection gives wrong remaining gaps and predicts non-zero rattle for the alternating case (which should be exactly zero).

Rattle LP

The maximum additional VV translation in direction θ from the assembly state is: 

  max  [cos θ, sin θ, 0] · δq
  s.t.  A·δq ≤ g⁺   (forward gap constraints)
       −A·δq ≤ g⁻   (backward gap constraints)

The rattle magnitude for a given assembly state is the maximum over all directions θ ∈ [0, 2π). This is solved via scipy.optimize.linprog with the HiGHS method.

Key insight: rattle depends on assembly state

Unlike the absolute LP bounds (which are fixed by geometry), the rattle from a given assembly state depends critically on how the supports landed:

  • Alternating state (u = [+1.5, −1.5, +1.5, …] mm): each adjacent pair of supports blocks motion in opposite directions. The VV is completely locked — rattle = 0.000 mm ✓.
  • Nominal state (u = 0 for all supports): symmetric gaps in both directions — rattle = 1.547 mm (equals the LP bound for the nominal case).
  • Worst-case assembly: supports pre-loaded in one direction free up gap in the opposite direction, allowing the VV to rattle further. MC P95 = 2.282 mm, worst observed = 2.896 mm.

The absolute upper bound on rattle is 2 × 1.547 mm = 3.094 mm (if the VV assembles at one extreme of the LP envelope, it can rattle to the opposite extreme). In practice this exact configuration cannot occur simultaneously for all supports, so the MC worst case (2.896 mm from 5000 samples) approaches but does not reach this limit.

Figure 1: Three assembly scenarios showing gap state (top row) and corresponding rattle envelope (bottom row). Nominal case: symmetric 1.547mm rattle. Alternating case: VV fully locked (0mm rattle). Worst-case MC sample: 2.896mm rattle using freed-up gaps.

§ Decisions

Which LP solver for bounding and rattle computation?

Verified numerically. Pseudoinverse BANNED — gives infeasible solutions (τ_max=2.086mm > 1.5mm limit). scipy HiGHS gives correct LP = 1.547mm and 0.000mm for alternating case.

How to model the assembly gap state for rattle LP?

Must use original uᵢ — they represent physical pin positions, not constrained to Col(A). lstsq projection gives wrong remaining gaps: alternating case gives ~0 for q₀, so formula predicts 1.547mm rattle instead of correct 0mm. Verified: correct LP gives 0.000mm ✓.
