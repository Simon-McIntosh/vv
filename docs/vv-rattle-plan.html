VV Rattle MC Analysis

§1 — Project Summary

The ITER Vacuum Vessel (VV) rests on 9 gravity supports equally spaced on a circle of radius R = 8 m. Each support constrains the VV in the toroidal direction only, with a gap of 3 mm total (±1.5 mm per support). The VV is therefore a rigid body with three in-plane degrees of freedom — dx, dy, and dθ — subject to 9 over-determined toroidal constraints solved via least-squares.

The assembly team needs bounding lateral displacement statistics to plan clearance checks and installation sequencing. Specifically:

  • What is the maximum possible lateral displacement of the VV in X and Y from nominal?
  • After the VV is assembled into a given position, how much additional rattle remains?
  • What is the probability distribution of assembly positions within the bounding envelope?

The deliverable is a concise bounding report with key numbers (mm), distribution plots, and clear guidance for assembly clearance requirements.

§3 — Physics Model

Rigid-body kinematics

The VV translates in the horizontal plane as a rigid body. Its configuration is described by three DOFs: lateral translation (dx, dy) in metres and rotation dθ in radians about the machine axis. The 9 gravity supports are located at angles φ_i = 2πi/9, i = 0, …, 8 on a circle of radius R = 8 m. The toroidal unit vector at support i is t̂_i = (−sin φ_i, cos φ_i).

The toroidal displacement of support i due to rigid-body motion is: 

  u_i = t̂_i · (dx, dy) + R · dθ

This gives a linear system A · [dx, dy, dθ]ᵀ = u where A is the 9×3 kinematic matrix. Because the system is over-determined the pseudo-inverse (lstsq) gives the best-fit rigid-body state for any realised gap vector u.

Full bounding envelope

The maximum achievable displacement in any direction is found by solving the linear programme: 

  maximise   c · [dx, dy, dθ]ᵀ
  subject to |u_i| ≤ 1.5 mm  for all i = 0, …, 8

bounding_box_analysis.py sweeps over objective directions c = ±ê_x, ±ê_y, ±ê_θ to produce the full reachable box. The result is the absolute worst-case envelope — any assembly state lies within it.

Assembly-condition rattle

After the VV is pinned into assembly position X₀ = (dx₀, dy₀, dθ₀) (realised by initial pin gaps u_i⁰), the remaining gap at support i is: 

  Δu_i⁺ = +1.5 mm − u_i⁰   (room to move in + direction)
  Δu_i⁻ = −1.5 mm − u_i⁰   (room to move in − direction)

The maximum additional translation from assembly state X₀ in the +X direction is then: 

  max_rattle_+X(X₀) = x_max − dx₀

where x_max is the bounding-box upper limit on dx. The total rattle range is: 

  rattle_range_X = x_max − x_min = constant (independent of assembly state)

This means the MC distribution characterises the probability that the VV assembles near a corner of the envelope (worst-case remaining rattle) versus near the centre (balanced remaining rattle).

§5 — Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1 — Assembly-condition MC module

Create vv_rattle_mc.py with a vectorized assembly Monte Carlo. Key functions:

  • build_kinematic_matrix(n=9, R=8.0) — returns 9×3 matrix A and its pseudo-inverse A⁺.
  • sample_assembly_positions(n_samples, tol=1.5e-3, rng=None) — draws (n_samples × 9) gap matrix, returns (n_samples × 3) assembly states via A⁺ @ u.T.
  • bounding_box(A, tol=1.5e-3) — LP-based envelope (wraps scipy.optimize.linprog).
  • rattle_statistics(X0_samples, bbox) — computes remaining-rattle distributions and worst-case values.
  • plot_assembly_distribution(X0_samples, bbox, outdir) — 2-panel figure: dx vs dy scatter + marginal PDFs with bounding box overlay.

Phase 2 — Run analysis, generate bounding plots

  • Run vv_rattle_mc.py with n_samples = 1_000_000.
  • Write plots/assembly_distribution.png and plots/rattle_cdf.png.
  • Print bounding numbers table to stdout and to results/bounding_numbers.txt.

Phase 3 — HTML report with embedded results

  • Create reports/vv_rattle_report.html — self-contained, base-64 plots, bounding table, physics summary section.
  • Readable without a web server; suitable for email distribution.

Phase 4 — Assembly team deliverable

  • Final review of numbers against independently calculated geometric bound.
  • Update main.py to orchestrate Phases 1–3 end-to-end.
  • Tag release, archive plots and report.

Which LP solver for the bounding box / conditional rattle computation?

LP solver for bounding analysis

Decision: Use scipy.optimize.linprog with HiGHS method for all envelope calculations. The pseudoinverse approach is BANNED — it produces physically infeasible solutions (violates support gap constraints by 24%).

Rationale: Verified numerically: pseudoinverse dx_max = 1.920 mm causes tau_max = 2.086 mm > 1.5 mm limit. True LP = 1.547 mm (feasible).
